Our cities are mostly experienced vertically — skylines, heights, rooflines. But there's another dimension we rarely see with our own eyes: the city's footprint. How much ground each building takes up, and what shape it leaves on the earth.

Maps show us the city from above — buildings in relation to streets, to each other, to the land they sit on. For this project, I wanted to strip all of that away. To pull each building out of its context and look at it alone. What can the shape of a building tell us when it has nothing around it? This is partly an investigation, partly an excuse to make something that looks cool.
